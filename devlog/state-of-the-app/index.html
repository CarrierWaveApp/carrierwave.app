2026-04-02 Behind the Scenes

State of the App

Carrier Wave has grown from a simple logging app into a full operating platform for amateur radio. With v1.79 out in the wild, we wanted to step back and take stock of where things stand — what's in the app today, what operators are getting the most out of, and where we're headed.

The big picture

When we started Carrier Wave, the goal was simple: stop duct-taping a dozen apps together just to log a QSO, upload to POTA, and confirm on LoTW. Two Extra Class operators tired of the same friction every other ham complains about.

Today, Carrier Wave connects to seven major services — QRZ, POTA, LoTW, Club Log, eQSL, HAMRS, and Ham2K LoFi — with bidirectional sync across all of them. You log a contact, and the data flows where it needs to go. No ADIF exports to manage, no manual uploads, no forgotten confirmations.

What's in the app right now

Here's an honest inventory of the major feature areas shipping today:

Logging and data

  • Quick Entry mode for rapid contact logging with color-coded token parsing
  • Full ADIF import and export for interop with every major platform
  • Per-service sync modes so you control exactly what goes where and when
  • iCloud backup and restore with Drive mirroring
  • A powerful search and filter system with its own query language

Activations

  • POTA activations with automatic uploads, rove mode, and park-to-park support
  • SOTA summit tracking
  • WWFF flora and fauna references
  • Agents on Air code exchange
  • Contest logging with WA7BNM calendar integration and Cabrillo export

Digital and CW

  • FT8 suite with live waterfall, auto-decoding, conversation tracking, and PSK Reporter integration
  • CW transcription with real-time Morse decoding via Goertzel filter
  • WebSDR integration with KiwiSDR receivers, recording, and instant replay

Radio control

  • BLE CAT control for Icom, Kenwood, Elecraft, and Yaesu rigs
  • Automatic frequency and mode tracking from your radio into the log
  • Mid-session equipment switching with the RIG command

Maps, spots, and propagation

  • Azimuthal map with antenna pattern overlays and spots heatmap
  • Smart Spot Needs — a background engine matching spots to your DXCC, WAS, and POTA needs
  • Band openings and DXpedition calendar

Sessions and sharing

  • Session timeline with map-based chronological replay
  • Video export for highlight reels with animated maps
  • Brag sheets and share cards for posting activations

Platform integration

  • Live Activity and Dynamic Island support for active sessions
  • watchOS companion with solar conditions, spots, and quick session start
  • Home screen widgets for solar data, spots, and stats
  • Equipment catalog with per-QSO rig tracking

What we've been working on lately

The last few releases have focused on depth over breadth. v1.78 brought the equipment catalog, activation recommendations, log scanning, FlexRadio support, and WebSDR instant replay. v1.79 followed up with per-service sync modes, a redesigned journal sync engine, and a widget refresh with activity type icons and SOTA spots.

On the documentation side, we've been building out a comprehensive manual with searchable, version-tracked docs for every feature in the app. If you've ever wondered how a specific feature works, the manual should have you covered.

The philosophy hasn't changed

We still build what we use. Every feature ships because one of us needed it at our own station — not because a spreadsheet said so. We test at parks, on summits, and in contests before anything goes out the door.

We only ship features we've personally tested at our own stations, on our own rigs, with our own callsigns on the air.

That's the bar, and it's not changing.

What's next

We're not going to lay out a public roadmap — we've learned that shipping when it's ready beats promising dates. But we will say this: the foundation is solid, the sync engine is maturing, and the features that are coming next build on everything above in ways we're genuinely excited about.

If you're using Carrier Wave, thank you. If you're curious, grab it from the App Store and come say hello on Discord.

73 de N9HO & W6JSV
